EyeModule Images

For Christmas 2000, Richard bought me an eyemodule for my handspring visor. This is a collection of images I've taken with it. I tend to add a new page whenever I have something worth sharing.

Update

Unfortunately on Thursday 26 July 2001, my eyemodule, visor and everything else I tend to carry around with me were stolen when my handbag was nicked from a pub (The Hogshead near Farringdon). So, at least for the moment, there will be no new pages added to this part of the site. Instead, we're posting pictures to the new photo gallery section.

As you will no doubt see from the images I've taken, I'm quite taken with the small black and white ones. I think they have some real mood about them. The quality isn't wonderful, but then the camera module is only 6cm long, 5cm wide and 2cm deep, so it's not exactly big to carry around.

Terminology help:
Handspring Visor - a Palm OS based computing device, it's a handheld and can be used to read mail, keep notes, addresses, appointments etc. There are also a load of third party applications available for it. See our review for more details.
Eyemodule - a clip on device to allow basic photographs to be taken with the handspring visor. See our review for more details.
