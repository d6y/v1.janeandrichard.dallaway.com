Sunday, April 07, 2002

Yesterday Steff and I met up at Oxford Circus for a spot of accessory shopping. Daisy is still not doing very well at the whole bottle feeding thing, so Simon and Daisy came too and went for some walks whilst we were in the shops.

We started off in Debenhams, and had really expected this to be a great place to start, as this was where Steff found all her stuff when she got married 2 years ago. But, no, it was really, really dissapointing. They seemed to not have a huge range of anything we were looking for, or even the things we weren't. We found nothing to interest us and so moved off.

Out next stop was House of Fraser. They seem to run a very strict appointments system, and it appeared that even to look at shoes, etc you needed to have an appointment to get anyones time. We decided it wasn't worth trying to arrange one and so moved on again.

Next we headed to John Lewis, and were surprised to find out that they no longer had a bridal department. They did, however, have some bridal shoes amongst their shoe section and I found a couple of pairs to try on - unfortuntately one pair didn't fit, and the other pair just looked weird. They also had the best lingerie department of the stores we looked at, and I even went and tried something on. At around this time Simon rang Steff to say that Daisy wanted her Mummy, and so we stopped for a cup of tea and a piece of cake.

Simon headed off again, and Steff and I headed along to BHS. This turned out to be one of the best bridal departments. They had quite a good selection of shoes, and we found some similar in style to one of the pairs in John Lewis, but £25 cheaper, and they fit really well. A purchase! We also had a good look at some of the adult bridesmaid dresses and got a few more ideas for Steff, and then looked at the pageboy waistcoasts and found one which will be pefect.

Richard had by this time arrived in London (we were going off to see Jono and Emma later) and so he came to meet us outside BHS and then Steff, Rich and I walked off to find Simon and Daisy. We headed off down Regent Street and Steff and I popped into Berketex Bride to look at other accessories (having sent the boys off to find a cafe for us to have a cup of tea together).

So, despite not buying much, I can tick another item off the todo list, and we've got a few more ideas now for other things. We also just had a generally good girlie shopping excuse - something that neither of us are the type to do without a good reason.
